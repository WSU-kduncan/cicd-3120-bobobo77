Basic Plot

There exists an group deep in the Antarctic that is known as Chaldea Security Organization. This organization's mission is to preserve humanity's future. To that extent, Magi have given Chaldeas the resources to see up to 100 years into the future to ensure Mankind can survive at least that long.

Chaldea has been given several tools to ensure extinction does not happen for humanity. Global Environmental Model Chaldeas: a magecraft view of the world. Near-Future Observation Lens Sheba: a mystery that can see into past and near future, and projects it onto Chaldeas. Heroic Spirit Summoning System Fate: A way to access the dimension known as the Throne of Heroes and summon people whom have left an impact on history, and materialize them in a known as a class container to aid in preserving humanity. Spiritron Calculation Engine Trimegistus: a technological and magecraft combination that can convert a human body or class container into spiritrons and send them to coordinates given through Sheba and Chaldeas. With these 4 pillars supporting Chaldea security Orginization, it looks like Humanity's future is safe and ensured.

Then, in 2017, the viewing abilities of Chaldea indicated that Humanity would be wiped out at the dawn of 2019. This viewing platform also detected a temporal anomaly known as a Singularity around a small Japanese town in the year 2004. Thanks in part to magecraft and science combining together to make Rayshifting, it is possible to send a spirit back in time and manifest a body for the spirit to interact with the world. You have been selected as one of 49 candidates who will partake in this mission to save humanity from extinction with the aid of historical and mythical figures at your command.

Gameplay

The mechanics of the game are quite easy to pick up, thanks in part to the tutorial which gives a taste of the combat system and the way you summon Servants. The story and events keep you from being bored and Start Dash Campaigns are an amazing way to keep drawing in newcomers.

Battle is a turn-based style that is easy to utilize, yet difficult to explain. You can command 6 summonable characters to go into a single combat: 3 starting off, and 3 to come in if any of the front line dies. Each "Servant" has five chances to attack over 3 turns, and you are given five randomly from the three in combat. There are three types of attacks: a damage focused attack, an attack that sets up for critical strikes the next round, and an attack that charges up your ultimate move for that respective character. each character also has 3 skills to affect combat to ensure that all combat isn't exactly the same. at level one, you usually get only one skill unlocked and have to level up your character to utilize all 3.

Leveling Servants is grindy, but satisfying. it is not done by sending these characters into battle, but rather through giving items to them to empower them. this is honestly the most tedious the game gets with needing to constantly repeat the same combat again and again to attain the materials needed thanks to atrocious drop rates.

Public Impact

To give you a comparison of how successful this game has been, Fate/Grand Order is consistently in the top 20 grossing games on mobile devices every quarter, and regularly hits the top 5 grossing each quarter. The popularity of the game is evident from sales and downloads alone.

Recently, there has been an anime series created for this game. This anime follows the plot of the game for only the 7th chapter, but it does not care if you haven't played the game too much, only that you have some knowledge of Fate beforehand.

This game has been so successful, I doubt there will be any end to this game for a long long time. It has earned more chatter than Fortnite on Twitter, and has the advantage of being playable even with low rarity Servants. Sony grossed more than $3 billion since launching in 2015. If this doesn't show the popularity of this game, I don't know what will.

Summoning

The main draw of the game is summoning historical figures who have left a lasting impact on history or myth. These beings each have massive strength and are the only way to actually fight all the enemies you encounter. To summon these beings, you must use a currency called "Saint Quartz" to summon them. It takes 3 Saint Quartz to do a summon. This is the main currency you can find, through quest completion, daily logins, and finishing weekly missions. This is also a freemium currency, meaning you can buy more with actual money. You are also given Saint Quartz after every maintenance for events, so keep that in mind.

Now, summoning can be done without Saint Quartz, and this is possible through Friend Points. You can get Friend Points by using supports. It is 10 points earned per stranger, 25 points per friend, and 200 points for a guest servant. While this seems like a lot, it is 200 points per roll on the friend point gacha. You will be given a choice of support Servants at every battle, but there are a few caveats. Strangers can become friends after a battle, but you can only have a limited number of friends at a time. also, Guest Servants are usually weaker than Friend supports or stranger supports.

There is one other way to summon Servants, and that is through Summon tickets. Summon tickets are the equivalent of a free summon on any of the saint quartz banners that you wish. You are given 1 summon ticket every time you log in 7 days in a row. These tickets are also available during events and Twitter/Facebook reward cycles.

General Opinion

This game is well-crafted and you see how much blood, sweat, and tears are put into this game to ensure a pleasing experience is given to all who play. The user interface is regularly updating to ensure new players and old players will enjoy their time playing. Events are usually a tad comedic and offer up amazing prizes to make these events engrossing.

In-game currency is plentiful and has multitudes of uses. Saint Quartz is what is used for sumooning characters, and can even be used to revive your team in battle. Daily login rewards and login milestones aid in accruing materials a bit faster than just grinding. The quests all give additional rewards which are more than welcome. even getting rid of Servants can help you because the remnants left behind can be traded for rare goodies.

Fate/Grand Order also can get people interested in other parts of the Nasuverse franchise. It has had 6 interactions with other stories from Kinoko Nasu's head. a lot of events also pad out your lineup with more potent Servants upon completion of the event. It is a game that knows how to get you hooked with an amazing story and keeps you with a gacha of not atrocious rates. All in all, I highly recommend the game for most people, just watch your wallet.
